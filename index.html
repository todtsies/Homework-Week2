About Me

My name is Emily. I grew up in central Virginia in a small town called Buckingham County. I graduated from Buckingham County High School in 2016 as 9th in my class. I participated in Governor's School, which allowed me to graduate from high school with my Associate's Degree. Out of high school I went to Virginia Tech, where I was majoring in Biological Sciences. After a year there, I realized that's not what I wanted to do with my life. Still unsure with what I wanted to do and not wanting to waste thousands of dollars, I moved back home and figured I'd get some perspective from the real working world. I began waiting tables and realized I loved working with people from all backgrounds. That's what made it fun! Now I work at a doggie daycare, which is the best job I've ever had. I'm able to take my dogs with my to work everyday! Who wouldn't love that?!

I was a tomboy as a child and absolutely loved to be outdoors. I played softball from the age of 9 to 18 and played the position of second base or short stop. I also played volleyball, I was a cheerleader and ran cross-country all four years of high school. I was apart of many clubs and associations in high school, like the National Honors Society, Beta Club, Ecology Club, Student Council Association, and more! I loved being involved in high school and plan to do the same in my career. Growing up, my family bred Labrador Retrievers. I think this is where my love for dogs came from. I have three dogs - one German Shepherd, one German Shorthaired Pointer, and one Siberian Husky. Their names are Titan, Voodoo, and Yeti. Yeti - the Siberian Husky - is a rescue that I met through my job at the doggie daycare. The previous owner was going to surrender him due to her financial situation. I knew him and my dogs got along great so I offered to take him in. It was the best decision I've made in a long time!
